1995 Jewish Life Grants

The grants listed here show the total amount of awards made in 1995, whether one year or multiyear, and the 1995 payments of multiyear grants awarded in a previous year. The grants reflect the program priorities in effect in 1995.

Jewish Education

BIBLIODRAMA training for Jewish educators, to teach them the use of role playing in Bible studies, Aleph: Alliance for Jewish Renewal, Philadelphia, PA, $10,000

MASTER TEACHER training to help classroom teachers improve the quality of supplementary school education, Council for Initiatives in Jewish Education, New York, NY, $225,000 over 3 years

EXPERIMENT in Congregational Education, a program to transform the synagogue culture of learning, Hebrew Union College-Jewish Institute of Religion, Los Angeles, CA, $45,000 (1995 payment)

MATERIALS on Jewish environmental values for students in Israel, Abraham Heschel Joshua School of Nature Studies, New Israel Fund, Washington, DC, $35,000

PEER COUNSELING and team teaching to encourage young people to continue their Jewish education, Princeton Center for Leadership Training, Princeton, NJ, $55,000

CIVICS and social justice instruction for Jewish high school students, Washington Institute for Jewish Leadership and Values, Rockville, MD, $35,000

Jewish Spirituality

JEWISH spirituality curriculum, Hebrew Union College-Jewish Institute of Religion, New York, NY, $75,000

EXPANSION of programs to bring the intermarried into the Jewish community, Jewish Outreach Institute, New York, NY, $65,000 (1995 payment)

VIDEO of the Tucson Artists-in-Residence Program, National Foundation for Jewish Culture, New York, NY, $7,200

JEWISH WISDOM and healing courses at ELUL to help people through difficult life passages, New Israel Fund, Washington, DC, $35,000

RABBINICAL training that emphasizes effective outreach skills, Reconstructionist Rabbinical College, Wyncote, PA, $55,000 (1995 payment)

RELIGION and television conference to promote a more positive depiction of religion on the networks, University of California, Los Angeles, CA, $20,000

TRANSFORMATION of synagogues into stronger communities of learning, healing, spirituality, and justice, Synagogue 2000, University of Judaism, Los Angeles, CA, $600,000 over 3 years

MENTORING program to foster the spiritual growth of rabbinic students, University of Judaism, Los Angeles, CA, $50,000 (1995 payment)
